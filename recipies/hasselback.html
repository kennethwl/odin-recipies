Hasselback potato from the gods.

All time classic potato preperation. Popular in America, but originated in northern Europe.

A small history lesson.

The name is derived from the restaurant where they were first introduced in the 1940s, Hasselbacken in Stockholm, Sweden which opened in that city in 1748 and where they are called hasselbackspotatis. Hasselback potatoes are a simple dish, and in their simplest form, are nothing more than whole potatoes cut in such a way as to resemble a fan or accordion when roasted. The outside of the potato becomes crisp and brown while the inside is soft and creamy.

Ingredients

  • 500 g Mary piper potato
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 50 ml good quallity olive oil
  • 50 ml bacon fat
  • 3 sticks fresh rosemary
  • 3 sticks lemon thyme
  • Fleur de sel
  • Cracked black pepper
  • 2 wooden sticks or spoons(i.e. chopsticks)

Instructions

  1. Put the two wooden sticks on spoons parralel next to eachother on the cutting board. Add the potato on top so that the bottem of the potato touches the board, but the rest rests on the sticks.
  2. Use a sharp knife to cut the potato in slices of 3-4 mm thick, stopping when the knife hits the wooden sticks. Repeat for all potatoes.
  3. Wash all the potatoes withc cold water, make sure you wash in between everey slice. This is an important step, else the slices will stick together in the oven because of the starch.
  4. Clean the garlic and cut this in razor thin slices, Goodfellas style.
  5. Put a slice of garlic in between every slice of potato.
  6. Preheat the oven to 450 F.
  7. Add the potatoes into a baking pan. And drizzle with half the olive oil and bacon fat.
  8. Add half of the herbs on top.
  9. Bake for 15 min.
  10. Get the potatoes out,brush off the herbs, add the rest of the oil and fat and add the rets of the herbs.
  11. Bake for another 15 min.
  12. Season the potatoes with fleur de sel and cracked black pepper. Et voila potatoes the way Odin ate them.
